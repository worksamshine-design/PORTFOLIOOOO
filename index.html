Project 01 · Branding · Product Design

Waou — Bespoke
Dog Accessory Brand

A luxury bespoke brand designed for dogs and their owners, blending emotional safety with high design and functionality.

The Brief

This project started from exploring brands and manufacturing types, coming across the term bespoke. Researching for a product niche where a luxury bespoke brand could be created — the moto was to create an emotional safety net for dogs while easing their owners' tasks too.

Project 03 · UX · Product Design

Kitsy First Aid Kits —
Redefining Emergency Readiness

A kid-friendly first aid kit designed for clarity, speed, and guided confidence in emergencies.

Problem Statement

Kids are unable to use basic first aid on their own without someone present to help them — resulting in small reactions turning worse, kid panicking, worried parents, and situations where expired medication causes greater problems.

Users don't need more tools — they need clarity, speed, and guidance during stressful emergency situations.

Project 04 · Research Paper · Inclusive Design

Sitting & Spacing Issues —
Elderly People on Local Trains

A research paper identifying spatial and accessibility gaps faced by elderly passengers on local trains, proposing human-centered design interventions.

Why This Topic?

Millions commute daily, but elderly passengers face severe accessibility gaps — high steps, lack of ergonomic seating, and overcrowded aisles create massive physical and emotional barriers. With an aging population, improving transport for elderly people can enhance their independence and quality of life.

This project involved 6 weeks of research through observations, interviews, surveys, and talking to railway workers.

Proposed Ideas

L-Shaped Seating — Redesigned seating incorporating individual armrests to provide necessary leverage for standing up and significantly better lumbar support.

Extended Handrails — Lowered handlebar extensions strategically placed to offer crucial grip and stability for passengers while the train is in rapid motion.

Accessible Storage — Lowered and repositioned luggage racks to ensure elderly passengers can easily stow and retrieve personal belongings without physical strain.

Lowered Handlebars — Low height hand bars at the top in the spaces between seats for standing.

Project 05 · Research · Product Design

The Recovery Paradox
& Lego Zen

An end-to-end HCD case study translating comprehensive post-workout qualitative research into a modular hot & cold field massager.

Introduction

This project addresses a critical contradiction in modern fitness — the Recovery Paradox. While biologically muscle growth and adaptation occur during rest, not during the workout itself, recovery is consistently the first activity people neglect when life gets busy.

Lego Zen is a multi-sensory, highly adaptive therapy device synthesized directly from research insights — engineered with magnet block modules that configure instantly to provide targeted heat, cold, and percussion therapy wherever an athlete needs it.
